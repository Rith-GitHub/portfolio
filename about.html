About Me

Currently I'm in my senior year at Dublin High School. I don't have a lot of hobbies, but I enjoy making new things and exercising my creativity. I practice playing the piano when I have free time and I also used to draw. Last year I began learning to cook so that I can be self-sustaining. I've been learning from my mom, but the majority of the recipes she's tried to teach me recently have been beyond my ability. At the very least, I can affirm that I possess basic skills required to meet my basic needs.

I find that I delight in creating, and consider that delight to be the reason why I pursue the hobbies that I do. Especially with composing music and drawing, the act of producing a work from my imagination is something I like doing. Trying to depict what I imagined, combining all the ideas stored in the mind is an amazing way to relax. And like many others, I have songs and music pieces that are my favorite to listen to, styles of art that I find outshine the rest, and cuisine that I love eating. I feel like trying to draw, compose, and cook have enriched these experiences for me. I feel a greater sense of amazement as I am able to comprehend the talent and effort of the creator.

For similar reasons I'm also interested in computer science. In middle school I wanted to learn how to code so that I could make video games. I took all sorts of courses and searched for any resources I could make use of. In my freshman year of high school I took Computer Science Principles. It was that class that changed the way I saw computer science. It was also through that class that I learned of my school's Engineering Academy, and became a member in 2017 (Here is the website of the academy ). In my sophomore year I took Principles of Engineering, which opened my mind up to other fields of engineering. When I became a junior I took AP Computer Science Applications to further expand my knowledge. This year, I am taking Digital Electronics to expand my scope to computer hardware and see whether the field is worth exploring further.
