Work and Volunteer Experience

Throughout the year various opportunities to expand my understanding of the engineering field were provided to me. It was through these opportunities that I was able to visualize more clearly what I should expect and prepare for in the future.

On a field trip to Cisco Systems we were shown what type of activities were done as workers of the company. I learned about the type of products they make and the design process that they go through to make sure the products are of good quality. Since Cisco Systems specializes in software and teleprescence equipment they also have to make sure whatever they make is secure and does not put their client at risk. We also got to see the different utilities they had in their workplaces so they could improve productivity from their workers.

A presentation from guest speakers that came to our class to talk about engineering licensing stood out to me because they of what they talked about in regards to the type of work that is done depending on your hierarchy in the workplace. Generally, as you rise to managment positions, the amount of work spent doing engineering tasks becomes less and the amount of time spent organizing people and managing products increases. They emphasized that as an engineer one had to be able to work with people and have the intention to have a positive effect on others. Knowing what to expect as an engineer helps narrow what future I want for myself.
